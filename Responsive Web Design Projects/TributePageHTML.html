ADOLF HITLER

The Man who killed 6 million Jews

This is the life history of Adolf Hitler

Early-life

1889: Born in Braunau am inn in Austria Hungary.

1895-1903: Attended elementary school in various locations displaying average academic performance. He showed an early interest in art and history, but his father wanted to him to pursue a career in civil service, much to Hitler's dismay.

1900: His younger brother, Edmund, died at the age of 6 which deeply affected him. He was closer to his mother, Klara, who provided him with emotional support.

1903: His father Alois dies.

1907: His mother dies from cancer.

Social-life

Family and relatives: His relationship with his father, Alois, was strained, as Alois was strict and often physically abusive.
Hitler was very close to his mother Klara, who doted on him and provided emotional support. Her death in 1907 deeply affected him.

Personal relationships and friends: Hitler had a few close friends and associates, including his early mentor, August Kuzibek, whome he met in his youth in Linz, Austria.
He also had a close relationship with Ernst Hanfstaengl, a Harvard-educated businessman who introduced him to influential circle in Munich.

Hobbies and interests:Hitler had a passion for art and aspired to be a painter in his youth. He spent so much time painting and sketching but his dream of becoming an artist never realized.
He was interested in architecture and was involved in the design of severa Nazi buildings and monuments.

Political-life

1919: Adolf Hitler joins the German workers party, later renamed National Socialist German workers Party (NSDAP), or Nazi Party.

1921: Adolf Hitler becomes the leader of the Nazi party.

1933: Appointed chancellor of Germany.

1933-1945: Adolf Hitler consolidated power, suspends civil liberties, and establishes a dictatorship.

1939: World war II begins with Hitlers expansionist policies. He is responsible for the death of approximately 6 million jews.

Death

April 30 1945: Adold Hitler dies in his undergroun bunker in Berlin, Germany.

Cause of death: Suicide by gunshot, alongside his wife Eva Braun who took cyanide

Circumstances: Occured as allied forced close in on Berlin during the final days of world war II in Europe. Hitlers body was found by his staff.
